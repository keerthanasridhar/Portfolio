"A Conceptual Design -Infinity Gauntlet"

Description

We had to choose a device used in a ScI-Fii movie or comic book or tv show to design a fictional interface in our Interface prototyping and design class and conceptualize and create an interactive interface.I chose the Infinity Gauntlet taking thanos as the user

USER ANALYSIS

TASK ANALYSIS

  • The infinity gauntlet granted virtual omnipotence to its wearer.
  • All the 6 stones are on the gauntlet so that they can be used for different purposes
  • The wearer can control the gauntlet depending on the needs of the wearer and hence the the interface needs to be control all the tasks.
  • More than one tasks can be choosen on the interface
Task 1 Time Gem: Controls over all aspects of time including slowing and accelerating the aging.
Task 2 Space Gem: Limitless manipulation of space allowing Teleportation, Interfaces wit motion of other objects.
Task 3 Soul Gem: controls the life and death, reverts the individuals natural state.
Task 4 Reality Gem: Alters the natural laws of the universe and can intervene between th matter and space.
Task 5 Mind Gem:Tap the user into the universal consiousness,allowing for unlimited manipulation and psionic abilites such as telekiness and empathy
Task 6 Power Gem: Controls strenght and durability and can enhance power from anything that holds power and gives power to other stones.

ENVIRONMENT ANALYSIS

  • The thing is that the Infinity Gauntlet cannot do its work outside the Universe where it comes from.
  • Its indestructible and so it can be used under any weather or climatic conditions.

Whiteboarding and Ideation

MindMapping,Chainstorming

Map was created to connect the user ,task and environment analysis of the user and create the functionality and use of the device.

I used mind mapping and chain storming technique for the individual brainstorming knowing the facts about the device.Mind mapping technique involves in creating a map between the device and the input and output and establish connection with the device.

Considering the way the device functions in the real comic and movies.

I considered Mind ,gestures ,screen and some unknown interface which I was trying to figure out how that would interact with the device.For gestures I thought of using unique gestures for each task or for each infinity gem and the user can use the gesture to invoke the power of the infinity gem.

The advantage of doing so is that all ideas were written down by not ignoring the small details and that helped me map the input interactions and the feedback mechanism.

After the brainstorming, came up with different interfaces

  1. Use of a horizontal touch bar at the end of the gauntlet that could show the options and so that the user gets to know how to operate it through a mental model.
  2. Using gestures and a screen: to record the gestures created by the user to enable the power of the infinity gem.
  3. Introducing the screen on the back of the hand to invoke all the options present for the interaction.
  4. Creating a knob with the mind stone, letting the mind stone gauge all the data and operate the knob to invoke all the power. Also with another knob outside to create a multi level hierarchy to choose the options given.
  5. Using touch on the gauntlet of each finger that consists the power can invoke the power of the infinity stone for multiple options.

What are the options to consider when the user has to choose the options for a particular task?
The low fidelity helped me iterate this process helped me decide how my screen interaction should happend and also made me understand the fucntionality of each task.

Affective Computing

"Observing the Impact of Music on the Affective State and Task Performance"

About:

This was my research project at school with two other fellow students.Our motivation for this study came from the idea that humans undergo different affective states when learning or completing a task. So it can then be assumed that particular affective states could yield different results when completing or learning a task, some better than others.

Approch:

Our approach for this study focused more so on whether there is a difference in overall performance, and whether this difference can be influenced using music.

  • Music is known to have an effect on the emotional states of people
  • From previous research we know that the tempo modulates the arousal value of the emotions (Fernandez-Sotos et al. 2016)
  • We can then assume that certain types of music can be used to evoke certain affective states for an individual
  • If applied, music with specific tempos can be used to influence the overall performance of individuals when completing task
  • Influence of Tempo and Rhythmic Unit in Musical Emotion Regulation
  • Alicia Fernández-Sotos, Antonio Fernández-Caballero, and José M.Latorre
  • This study was able to demonstrate a change in the test subjects’ overall emotional state by using music with various tempo and rhythm. They were able to conclude that: “..the tempo modulates the arousal value of emotions”
  • High Tempo - Stressing, Expressive, Amusing (+ Motivation)
  • Low Tempo - Relaxing, Expressionless, Boring (- Motivation)

Methodology for testing:

Technology Used for Testing

  • Desktop PC / Macbook Pro - for game playing
  • Apple Watch - to measure heartbeat
  • Tetris - to provide a task
  • Youtube - music source

Environment

  • User Studies Lab, Rm 442
  • Quiet environment with minimal distraction

Music

During observation, we chose instrumental music to eliminate the possibility of lyrics influencing the affective states of the participant. This helped us placed more emphasis on the music bpm.

  • No Music (0 bpm)
  • The Fugees - Fu Gee La (90 bpm, representing low arousal)
  • Linkin Park - Don’t Stay (180 bpm, representing high arousal)

Data Collection

During the testing phase game data was recorded in order to generalize the participant’s performance level. Physiological data was also gathered in order to determine the overall levels of arousal experienced by the participants.

  • Start Time
  • End Time
  • Score
  • Heartbeat bpm Min. (starting)
  • Heartbeat bpm Max.
  • Facial Expressions - recorded any apparent facial frustration or reactions to gameplay
  • Post Questionnaire Survey

Results

  • Between changing Heart Rate along with the difference between the score with music Bpm(0,90,150 Bpm)
  • Considering the heart rate(min and max) and score, we took the changes of heart rate with each categories to understand the correlation.
  • And used scatter plot to plot the correlation of three categories such as; 0 - 90 Bpm , 90-150Bpm and 0 -150 Bpm.
  • To understand the perfect fit between the data and the line we used r2 on the trend line.

Usability Evaluation

Redesign

PROJECT DESCRIPTION

This project helped conceptualizing and understanding the fundamental human-computer interaction issues, as well as user testing and interaction design processes.

ABOUT

The main purpose for conducting the usability test for the Create Art center is to address the client goals for the website.Another Reason included is to evaluate the possible problems faced during the usability of the website and improve the user experience of the website. During this process, I documented the information with user analysis along with environmental analysis.

Design Research Study

Steps Description
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS Compare sites with similar purpose to study the difference and improve the negatives.Comparison was done with various sites with considering the same background to understand how differently other sites showed the purpose of the website.Also,how the Usability problems were tackled.
CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY The contextual Inquiry involved brieﬂy interviewing random participants and observed them using the site by giving them certain task to perform.
Method Involved Using a script to brief the process that involved transition to task methodology.While the participants were performing the task observation was done to gauage the process of the participants.
CLIENT INTERVIEW: Executive Director from the Create Arts Center represented being the client of the organiza-tion giving me information of the stakeholders goals, targeted audience, prospective audience, perception on how the website could be improved and what are his expectations.
USER INTERVIEW Two Random participants who were supposed to be recruited being the prospective user for the web-site who could do the evaluation and ﬁnd out the usability errors in the website

TASK ANALYSIS

Hierarchical task analysis shows the task performed by the user using the website by the evalu-ator. The Evaluator chooses ﬁve task and walk through each task to create a step by step list of stages to understand the usability function or issues they face during the process.

PERSONA CREATION

Personas were created to get an idea about the prospective users using the website based on the information given by the client.

HUERISTIC EVALUATION

Heuristic evaluation was done by two evaluators guided by a list of guidelines and as to how to ﬁnd the issues of the website and give it a severity rating to understand the issue in a big-ger picture.

PARTICIPATORY DESIGN

Participatory design is a method of collaboration between the participant and the researcher and involving maximum involvement from the user.

BRAINSTROMING IDEAS

Think Aloud

Think Aloud method basically gave an understanding of what the user actually think of the de-sign.

Medium Fidelity Prototype

A medium ﬁdelity prototype is a prototype with limited functionality but clickable areas which presents the interactions and navigation possibilities of an application

Usability Study

Usability study was done to evaluate the interface which was both the prototype and the web-site.Usability study was done in such a way that the researcher ﬁnds out the frustration and deadlocks of website used by the user completing a particular task.

EYE TRACKING

Eye tracking is the measurement of eye activity. Where do we look? What do we ignore? When do we blink? How does the pupil react to different stimuli?. There are many different methods of exploring eye data
